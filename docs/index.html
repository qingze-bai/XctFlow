Abstract



Our key insight is to incorporate geometric constraints into the generative models. In this paper, we present the XctFlow, a novel conditional flow matching framework for reconstructing CT images from orthogonal biplane X-ray radiographs, which simulates the parallel projection process to extract 3D anatomical priors from radiographs and subsequently reconstructs these priors into accurate CT representations through a deterministic and differentiable continuous probabilistic path.

To simulate parallel projection, we proposed orthogonal constraint-based projection reconstruction module (OrthoPRM), which ensures geometric consistency and prevents distortions by focusing only on the 3D anatomical structures related to the corresponding pixels. Furthermore, we introduce orthogonality loss as a regularization term to improve the simulation of the parallel beam sampling process. Building upon CNF, the reconstruction process of XctFlow constructs a differentiable continuous probability path between anatomical priors and accurate CT representations.

Experimental results demonstrate that XctFlow achieves state-of-the-art performance, excelling in both anatomical detail and image quality. Benefiting from this improved performance, we extend XctFlow to two downstream tasks, organ segmentation and body composition analysis, showcasing its potential in various medical applications.
